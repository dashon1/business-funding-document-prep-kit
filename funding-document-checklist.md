# Funding Document Checklist

**Your Complete Guide to Every Document You'll Need for Business Funding**

---

## Why This Checklist Matters

Most business owners scramble for documents at the last minute, looking disorganized and unpolished to lenders. This checklist ensures you have everything ready before you need it—so you approach funding opportunities with confidence and professionalism.

Lenders make decisions quickly. When your documents are organized and complete, you stand out from the crowd of unprepared applicants.

---

## Document Categories

### Category 1: Business Identity Documents

These establish your business as a legitimate entity.

| Document | Why Lenders Need It | Status |
|----------|---------------------|--------|
| Articles of Incorporation | Proves your business exists legally | ☐ |
| Business License | Shows you're authorized to operate | ☐ |
| EIN Confirmation Letter | Identifies your business for tax purposes | ☐ |
| DBA (Doing Business As) Filings | Confirms any alternate business names | ☐ |
| Operating Agreement or Bylaws | Demonstrates your governance structure | ☐ |

**Action Step:** Make copies of all identity documents and store them in your "Business Basics" folder.

---

### Category 2: Financial Statements

These show the numbers behind your business.

| Document | Why Lenders Need It | Status |
|----------|---------------------|--------|
| Profit & Loss Statement (P&L) | Shows income, expenses, and net profit | ☐ |
| Balance Sheet | Displays assets, liabilities, and equity | ☐ |
| Cash Flow Statement | Demonstrates how money moves through your business | ☐ |
| Income Tax Returns (2-3 years) | Verifies reported income with official records | ☐ |
| Accounts Payable Aging Report | Shows money you owe to vendors | ☐ |
| Accounts Receivable Aging Report | Shows money owed to you by customers | ☐ |

**Action Step:** Prepare your last 12 months of P&L statements and your most recent balance sheet.

---

### Category 3: Bank Documentation

These prove your business has legitimate banking relationships.

| Document | Why Lenders Need It | Status |
|----------|---------------------|--------|
| Business Bank Statements (12 months) | Shows your cash flow patterns and account health | ☐ |
| Business Checking Account Confirmation | Verifies your primary business account | ☐ |
| Savings/Money Market Statements | Shows reserves and financial stability | ☐ |
| Merchant Services Statements | Demonstrates card processing revenue | ☐ |

**Action Step:** Request official copies from your bank or download from online banking. See the Bank Statement Prep Sheet for tips on presenting these effectively.

---

### Category 4: Debt Documentation

These show your existing financial obligations.

| Document | Why Lenders Need It | Status |
|----------|---------------------|--------|
| Loan Statements (all current loans) | Shows existing debt and payment history | ☐ |
| Credit Card Statements (business) | Shows credit utilization | ☐ |
| Lines of Credit Statements | Shows available credit capacity | ☐ |
| Vendor Financing Agreements | Shows any trade credit arrangements | ☐ |
| Leasing Agreements | Shows equipment or property obligations | ☐ |

**Action Step:** List every debt with current balance, interest rate, and monthly payment. Use the Debt Overview Worksheet to organize this information.

---

### Category 5: Revenue Verification

These prove your business actually makes money.

| Document | Why Lenders Need It | Status |
|----------|---------------------|--------|
| Sales/Invoice Records | Shows your revenue volume and patterns | ☐ |
| Contracts with Major Customers | Demonstrates recurring revenue stability | ☐ |
| Point-of-Sale Reports | Verifies cash-based revenue | ☐ |
| Payment Processor Records | Shows online transaction volume | ☐ |
| Tax Records Showing Revenue | Cross-verifies income claims | ☐ |

**Action Step:** Pull your last 12 months of sales data and organize by month. Use the Revenue Snapshot Sheet to format this data for lenders.

---

### Category 6: Collateral Documentation

These support secured loan applications.

| Document | Why Lenders Need It | Status |
|----------|---------------------|--------|
| Equipment Titles/Ownership Docs | Shows assets you can pledge as collateral | ☐ |
| Real Estate Deeds/Mortgage Statements | Shows property ownership | ☐ |
| Vehicle Registration (business vehicles) | Shows transportation assets | ☐ |
| Inventory Records | Shows current stock value | ☐ |
| Investment Account Statements | Shows additional assets | ☐ |

**Action Step:** List all business assets with estimated current value. Note which ones are already pledged as collateral on existing loans.

---

### Category 7: Personal Financial Documents

Lenders often require personal guarantees from business owners.

| Document | Why Lenders Need It | Status |
|----------|---------------------|--------|
| Personal Tax Returns (2-3 years) | Shows your overall financial picture | ☐ |
| Personal Bank Statements (3-6 months) | Shows personal cash flow | ☐ |
| Personal Balance Sheet | Summarizes your assets and liabilities | ☐ |
| Real Estate Holdings Documentation | Shows property equity you could leverage | ☐ |
| List of Other Business Interests | Discloses potential conflicts or assets | ☐ |

**Action Step:** Gather your personal financial documents separately. Store them in a "Personal Finance" folder alongside your business documents.

---

### Category 8: Business Plan & Strategy Documents

These show lenders you have a clear plan.

| Document | Why Lenders Need It | Status |
|----------|---------------------|--------|
| Business Plan (executive summary) | Shows you have thought through your business | ☐ |
| Use of Funds Description | Explains exactly how you'll use the financing | ☐ |
| Cash Flow Projections (12 months) | Demonstrates ability to repay the loan | ☐ |
| Market Analysis | Shows you understand your industry | ☐ |
| Competitive Analysis | Shows you know your position in the market | ☐ |

**Action Step:** Complete the Use of Funds Worksheet to clearly articulate your funding goals.

---

## Quick Reference: Most Common Documents Lenders Request

In order of frequency requested:

1. **Business Tax Returns (2-3 years)**
2. **Personal Tax Returns (2-3 years)**
3. **Profit & Loss Statement (last 12 months)**
4. **Balance Sheet (current)**
5. **Business Bank Statements (last 6-12 months)**
6. **Debt Schedule (all current obligations)**
7. **Accounts Receivable Aging**
8. **Accounts Payable Aging**
9. **Business Plan with Use of Funds**
10. **Personal Financial Statement**

## Document Organization Tips

- Create a master folder structure (see Folder Organization Guide)
- Keep both physical copies and digital backups
- Update documents quarterly before you need them
- Use consistent naming conventions for digital files
- Store sensitive financial documents securely
