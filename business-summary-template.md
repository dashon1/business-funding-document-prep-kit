# Business Summary Template

**A Professional One-Page Overview That Lenders Love**

---

## Purpose of This Document

This business summary gives lenders a quick, comprehensive overview of your company. It should be concise enough to read in two minutes but informative enough to answer their most important questions. A strong business summary demonstrates that you understand your business and can communicate clearly—qualities that lenders value.

Complete this template once, then update it quarterly as your business evolves. Lenders appreciate receiving current information from organized business owners.

## Tips for Completing This Template

**Be specific.** Lenders appreciate concrete data over vague statements. Instead of "We serve many customers," say "We serve 340 active customers with an average annual value of $2,400."

**Be honest.** Present your business accurately. Lenders will verify your claims—exaggerations destroy trust and your credibility.

**Be current.** Update this summary at least quarterly. Outdated information suggests inattention to detail.

**Be concise.** Every section should provide value without unnecessary elaboration. Lenders appreciate efficiency.

**Be professional.** Proofread carefully. Typos and errors suggest carelessness.

## Customization Examples

**For a Retail Business:**
Focus on foot traffic, inventory turnover, location advantages, and customer demographics.

**For a Service Business:**
Emphasize recurring revenue models, client retention rates, service quality metrics, and team expertise.

**For a Manufacturing Business:**
Highlight production capacity, equipment quality, supplier relationships, and quality certifications.

**For an E-commerce Business:**
Show sales channels, conversion rates, customer acquisition costs, and return customer rates.

## Review Checklist

Before sharing your business summary, verify:

- [ ] All financial figures match your tax returns and statements
- [ ] Customer counts and percentages are accurate
- [ ] Years in business and experience claims are verifiable
- [ ] Market size and growth claims have reasonable support
- [ ] Competitor names and descriptions are current
- [ ] Contact information is accurate
- [ ] Grammar and spelling are perfect
- [ ] Format is clean and professional
- [ ] Length is appropriate (1-2 pages maximum)
