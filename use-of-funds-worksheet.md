# Use of Funds Worksheet

**Clearly Explain How You'll Use Your Funding—and Why Lenders Love Detailed Plans**

---

## Why This Worksheet Matters

Lenders don't just want to know that you need money—they want to understand exactly how you'll use it and how the investment will generate returns. A vague answer like "working capital" signals uncertainty. A detailed use-of-funds plan demonstrates strategic thinking and gives lenders confidence in your ability to manage their money responsibly.

This worksheet guides you through creating a complete, compelling explanation of your funding request.

## Quick Reference: Common Use of Funds Categories

Use these categories as a starting point, then customize for your specific situation:

**Growth/Expansion:**

- New location buildout: $50,000 - $500,000+
- Hiring additional staff: $30,000 - $200,000/year
- Marketing campaigns: $5,000 - $100,000
- Equipment purchase: $10,000 - $1,000,000+
- Inventory expansion: $10,000 - $500,000

**Operations:**

- Working capital: $10,000 - $500,000
- Seasonal inventory: $20,000 - $200,000
- Accounts receivable financing: Variable based on AR
- Vendor payments/supply chain: $15,000 - $100,000

**Refinancing:**

- Consolidate high-interest debt
- Pay off expensive credit cards
- Buy out business partners
- Pay off existing loans early
