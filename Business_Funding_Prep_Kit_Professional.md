## SECTION 2: BANK STATEMENT PREP SHEET

### How to Organize and Present Your Business Bank Statements

---

#### What Lenders Are Actually Looking For

When a lender reviews your bank statements, they're evaluating five key factors:

**1. Average Daily Balance**

This is the average amount of money in your account over the statement period. Lenders calculate this by adding up each day's balance and dividing by the number of days.

*What they're looking for:* Higher average balances suggest financial stability. They want to see balances that would cover your proposed monthly loan payment multiple times over.

*Pro tip:* Review your last 12 months of statements and note any months where the average balance dropped significantly. Be prepared to explain these periods.

**2. Cash Flow Consistency**

Lenders analyze the patterns in your deposits. Consistent, regular deposits from customers suggest reliable revenue. Highly variable deposits raise concerns about income stability.

*What they're looking for:* Predictable deposit patterns that match your described business model.

*Pro tip:* Create a deposit pattern analysis showing lenders what "normal" looks like for your business.

**3. Expense Management**

Lenders look at how you manage expenses. Large, unusual withdrawals or consistent overspending can signal poor financial management.

*Pro tip:* Categorize your major expenses on a separate sheet. When you can explain where money goes, you look more competent.

**4. Balance Trends**

The overall direction of your balances matters. Growing balances suggest a healthy, profitable business. Declining balances raise concerns.

*Pro tip:* Calculate your month-over-month balance change. If you've grown by $5,000 over the year, highlight this.

**5. Red Flags**

Lenders train themselves to spot warning signs. Overdrafts, large unexplained transfers, or suspicious activity can kill your application.

*Pro tip:* If you see red flags in your history, address them proactively. Explain what happened and what you changed.

---

#### Step-by-Step Preparation Process

##### Step 1: Gather Complete Statements

**Required:** Last 12 months of business checking account statements

**Preferred format:** Official PDFs downloaded directly from your bank

**What to include:**

- All pages of each statement (statements often span multiple pages)
- Any notices or disclosures included with statements
- Overdraft notices or fee disclosures

**What NOT to include:**

- Personal bank accounts (keep separate)
- Statements from closed accounts (include separately if requested)
- Screenshots or photos of statements

##### Step 2: Create a Statement Summary

For each month, record these key metrics:

| Month | Beginning Balance | Ending Balance | Total Deposits | # of Deposits | Largest Deposit | Total Withdrawals | # of Withdrawals |
|-------|-------------------|----------------|---------------|---------------|-----------------|-------------------|------------------|
| Jan | $ | $ | $ | | $ | $ | |
| Feb | $ | $ | $ | | $ | $ | |
| Mar | $ | $ | $ | | $ | $ | |
| Apr | $ | $ | $ | | $ | $ | |
| May | $ | $ | $ | | $ | $ | |
| Jun | $ | $ | $ | | $ | $ | |
| Jul | $ | $ | $ | | $ | $ | |
| Aug | $ | $ | $ | | $ | $ | |
| Sep | $ | $ | $ | | $ | $ | |
| Oct | $ | $ | $ | | $ | $ | |
| Nov | $ | $ | $ | | $ | $ | |
| Dec | $ | $ | $ | | $ | $ | |

##### Step 3: Calculate Your Average Monthly Balance

**Formula:** (Sum of all daily balances) ÷ (Number of days in period)

**Or simplified:** (Beginning balance + Ending balance) ÷ 2 for each month, then average all months

**Example:**

- January: Beginning $15,000, Ending $18,000 → Average: $16,500
- February: Beginning $18,000, Ending $17,000 → Average: $17,500
- March: Beginning $17,000, Ending $22,000 → Average: $19,500

**12-Month Average:** $17,833

**Use this calculation:** If your average monthly balance is $17,833 and your proposed loan payment would be $1,200/month, your balance covers the payment nearly 15 times. This demonstrates strong repayment capacity.

##### Step 4: Create Deposit Pattern Analysis

Document where your deposits come from:

| Deposit Source | Monthly Amount | % of Total | Pattern |
|----------------|---------------|------------|---------|
| Customer A (recurring contract) | $ | % | Monthly |
| Customer B (invoices) | $ | % | Bi-weekly |
| E-commerce sales | $ | % | Weekly |
| Retail sales | $ | % | Daily |
| Other | $ | % | |

**Key Insight:** If one customer represents more than 30% of your deposits, note this concentration risk. Lenders worry about over-reliance on single customers.

##### Step 5: Create Explanatory Notes

For any unusual items, prepare explanations:

**Large Single Deposits:**

- Date: ____________
- Amount: $ ____________
- Source: ____________
- Explanation: ____________

**Large Withdrawals:**

- Date: ____________
- Amount: $ ____________
- Purpose: ____________
- Explanation: ____________

**Low Balance Periods:**

- Month: ____________
- Balance: $ ____________
- Cause: ____________
- Resolution: ____________

##### Step 6: Assemble Your Package

**Organize in this order:**

1. Cover page with account summary
2. 12-month statement summary table
3. Average balance calculations
4. Deposit pattern analysis
5. Explanatory notes (if needed)
6. Individual statements (most recent first)
