## SECTION 2: BANK STATEMENT PREP SHEET

### How to Organize and Present Your Business Bank Statements

---

#### Understanding What Lenders Look For

**Primary Factors Lenders Evaluate:**

**Average Daily Balance:** Lenders calculate your average balance over the statement period. Higher average balances suggest financial stability and the ability to cover loan payments. They typically want to see at least 2-3 months of average balances that would cover your proposed monthly payment.

**Cash Flow Patterns:** They look for consistency in deposits. A business with highly variable deposits may be considered higher risk. Regular, predictable deposits from customers suggest stable revenue.

**Expense Management:** Lenders notice large or unusual withdrawals. They want to see that you're managing expenses responsibly and not living beyond your means.

**Overdraft History:** Frequent overdrafts signal poor cash management and raise red flags for lenders.

---

#### Step-by-Step Preparation Process

##### Step 1: Gather Your Statements

Collect the last 12 months of business bank statements. If applying for a smaller loan, 6 months may suffice, but 12 months provides the most comprehensive picture.

**Where to get them:**

- Download from your online banking portal
- Request official copies from your bank branch
- Access through your bank's mobile app
- Use your bank's customer service line

**Format considerations:**

- PDF format is preferred for digital applications
- Official bank copies with the bank's logo and letterhead are best
- Avoid screenshots lenders want authentic documentation

##### Step 2: Review Each Statement

Before presenting your statements, review them critically. Look for:

**Deposits to highlight:**

- Regular customer payments
- Recurring revenue streams
- Large contracts or one-time payments

**Items to explain:**

- Large irregular withdrawals
- Transfers to personal accounts
- Large payments to vendors
- Any deposits that might look unusual

Create a brief note for each statement explaining any items that might raise questions. Transparency builds trust.

##### Step 3: Organize by Date Range

Structure your statement package chronologically, most recent first. Each statement should be complete and include all pages.

##### Step 4: Create a Summary Page

Add a one-page summary that lenders can review quickly:

- Bank name and account number (last 4 digits only)
- Account type (checking, savings, money market)
- Average monthly balance
- Highest balance in the period
- Lowest balance in the period

##### Step 5: Highlight Positive Patterns

When your bank statements show healthy patterns, make them obvious. Create a separate highlights page that calls attention to:

- Growing balances over time
- Stable revenue patterns
- Cash reserves above operating expenses

#### Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake 1: Submitting outdated documents**

Lenders want statements from the last 30-90 days, not from six months ago.

**Mistake 2: Missing pages**

Bank statements often span multiple pages. Ensure every page is included.

**Mistake 3: Inconsistent business names**

Your legal name, DBA, and bank account name must align or be explained.

**Mistake 4: Forgetting personal guarantees**

If you personally guarantee the loan, include your personal financial documents.

**Mistake 5: Unclear use of funds**

"Lump sum" and "working capital" are vague. Be specific about exactly what the money will accomplish.
